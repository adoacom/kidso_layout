Rethinking "The Playroom"
CATEGORY:PARTY /DATE:Jan 23, 2015
As expecting parents, we are ripe with wonderful dreams about what having a child will be like. We anticipate and prepare for our baby in every way possible. Then our babies arrive and we learn that reality and our dreams don't always align. In American culture, we have a concept of "The Playroom". A playroom is a room, in the home, specifically designated and designed for child's play. The concept of a playroom is often one of those warm, fuzzy images expecting parents love to dream about, but the reality of a room filled with toys and children can be more of a headache than we imagine. The following article will debunk the myth of the "catalog" styled playroom and will offer a less expensive and more developmentally appropriate approach to play at home.
